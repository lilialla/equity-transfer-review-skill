# Contract Review Playbook

Use this reference when the user provides an equity transfer agreement, share transfer agreement, supplemental agreement, shareholder agreement, investment agreement, articles of association, or clauses that govern transfer restrictions.

## Review Method

Build a clause-by-clause issue list. For each issue, state:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Risk point | What exactly is wrong, missing, ambiguous, or unsupported. |
| Transaction impact | How it affects signing, closing, registration, payment, valuation, control, liability, or enforceability. |
| Revision direction | What must be added, removed, narrowed, conditioned, or moved to a closing deliverable. |
| Example wording | Provide sample language only when the facts are sufficient; otherwise give drafting instructions. |
| Supplemental document | Name the document, provider, timing, and completion evidence. |

## Clauses To Check

1. Parties and authority: identity, registration details, signatory authority, spouse or internal approval, power of attorney, board or shareholder approval.
2. Subject equity or shares: company name, registered capital, transfer percentage, corresponding subscribed and paid-in capital, share class, encumbrances, beneficial ownership, and disclosure of indirect arrangements.
3. Price and payment: valuation basis, total price, unit price, currency, payment account, installments, escrow or supervised account, tax gross-up, withholding, price adjustment, earn-out, holdback, and refund.
4. Taxes and fees: income tax, stamp duty, VAT if relevant, filing or declaration obligations, responsible party, timing, evidence, and consequences of non-cooperation.
5. Contribution liability: subscribed/paid-in status, contribution deadline, defective or withdrawn contribution, non-cash contribution valuation, pre-closing cure, post-closing contribution calls, creditor exposure, price deduction, holdback, and special indemnity.
6. Conditions precedent: corporate approvals, other shareholder consent or waiver, regulatory approval or filing, third-party consent, release of pledge or freeze, payment or cure of historical capital contributions, tax or FX completion evidence, disclosure confirmation, and no material adverse change.
7. Closing mechanics: closing date, closing deliverables, handover standard, payment-registration sequence, tax filing sequence, title transfer moment, risk transfer moment, post-closing registration covenant, and long-stop date.
8. Registration and public filing: market-regulation registration, shareholder register, articles amendment, UBO or controller records if applicable, listed or NEEQ disclosure, foreign-invested enterprise filing where relevant.
9. Representations and warranties: ownership, no encumbrance, no undisclosed nominee holding, full disclosure, valid approvals, no litigation or enforcement restriction, tax compliance, capitalization and contribution status, business permits, sanctions or compliance status if relevant.
10. Disclosure: schedule of exceptions, known disputes, debts, capital contribution defects, preferential rights, employee stock platform arrangements, related-party arrangements, regulatory issues, and pending litigation.
11. Interim period obligations: business conduct, no additional encumbrance, no capital increase or disposal, no dividends, information access, cooperation with approvals, tax/FX/bank review cooperation, and preservation of permits and assets.
12. Default, indemnity, termination: objective triggers, cure period, liability cap or carve-out, special indemnity for historical defects or contribution exposure, termination right, survival period, dispute costs, and evidence of loss.
13. Confidentiality, notices, governing law, and dispute resolution: compatible with transaction timetable, parties' actual addresses, service rules, interim measures, arbitration or litigation venue, and enforcement practicality.

## Redrafting Discipline

Do not produce generic comments. Convert each legal point into a transaction control:

- Condition precedent.
- Closing deliverable.
- Escrow or holdback.
- Disclosure schedule.
- Special indemnity.
- Warranty survival.
- Price adjustment.
- Termination right.
- Post-closing covenant.
- Pre-closing remediation.
- Tax withholding or filing evidence.
- Bank, FX, or escrow release condition.
- Contribution cure, holdback, or special indemnity.

If the agreement is missing core facts, stop drafting precise language and request the missing deal facts first.
