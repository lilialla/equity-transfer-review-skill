# Risk Taxonomy

Use this reference to classify issues and choose transaction controls.

## Severity

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 重大 | Likely blocks signing, closing, registration, legality, control acquisition, payment safety, or enforceability unless resolved. |
| 较高 | May materially affect price, liability allocation, approval path, timing, or post-closing dispute exposure. |
| 中等 | Needs revision, disclosure, covenant, or supporting document, but normally can be handled through transaction documents. |
| 低 | Drafting, process, or evidence issue with limited transaction impact. |
| 信息缺口 | Facts or documents are insufficient to determine the risk; request targeted materials. |

## Risk Categories

### Ownership And Disposition

- Transferor is not the registered shareholder or cannot prove beneficial ownership.
- Nominee holding, trust, co-ownership, spouse property, inheritance, enforcement, bankruptcy, pledge, freeze, seizure, or dispute affects disposition.
- Subscribed capital, paid-in capital, non-monetary contribution, defective contribution, capital withdrawal, or historical transfer payment affects value or liability.
- Target equity is subject to employee incentive, repurchase, VAM, lock-up, or special rights.

Possible controls: release document, waiver, spouse consent, administrator consent, escrow, special indemnity, title warranty, condition precedent, or pre-closing remediation.

### Contribution Liability And Valuation

- Unpaid subscribed capital, accelerated contribution, defective contribution, non-cash contribution valuation, withdrawn contribution, or unclear contribution obligation may affect value, transfer price, creditor exposure, and liability allocation.
- The agreement does not allocate responsibility for pre-closing contribution defects, post-closing calls for capital, creditor claims, or contribution-related registration corrections.
- Transfer price does not reflect unpaid or defective contribution exposure.

Possible controls: contribution cure before closing, price deduction, escrow or holdback, special indemnity, transferor warranty survival, disclosure schedule, creditor claim reserve, or termination right if contribution exposure exceeds an agreed threshold.

### Transfer Restrictions And Approvals

- Articles, shareholder agreement, investment agreement, or side letter contains consent right, preemptive right, co-sale, drag-along, lock-up, no-transfer covenant, control-change trigger, repurchase right, board or shareholder approval, or investor veto.
- Other shareholders' consent, notice, waiver, or preemptive-right exercise evidence is missing.
- Internal approval, signatory authority, power of attorney, or corporate authorization is incomplete.

Possible controls: prior notice package, written waiver, formal resolution, updated articles, legal confirmation, long-stop date, or termination right.

### Agreement Mechanics

- Subject equity, price, payment path, tax allocation, closing sequence, registration duty, deliverables, or title-transfer moment is ambiguous.
- Reps and warranties do not cover ownership, encumbrance, approvals, disclosure, litigation, tax, permits, or historical defects.
- Default, indemnity, termination, survival, confidentiality, notice, or dispute resolution does not match transaction risk.

Possible controls: clause revision, disclosure schedule, closing checklist, holdback, escrow, indemnity cap carve-out, warranty survival period, and dispute forum adjustment.

### Regulatory And Third-Party Requirements

- State-owned asset approval, appraisal, exchange listing, filing, or public transaction procedure may be required.
- Foreign-investment access, security review, foreign exchange, overseas investment, or cross-border structure issues exist.
- Listed-company, NEEQ, fund, financial institution, education, medical, internet, data, or cybersecurity restrictions apply.
- Merger control, national security review, data export, or industry permit change may be triggered.
- Bank, creditor, lender, major customer, lessor, license issuer, or platform consent is required.

Possible controls: official-source verification, regulatory memo, condition precedent, no-filing analysis, filing timetable, third-party consent, disclosure, and closing deferral.

### Tax, Payment And FX Completion

- Natural person, entity, non-resident, low-price transfer, related-party transfer, indirect transfer, installment payment, escrow, or cross-border payment creates tax, withholding, stamp duty, foreign exchange, or bank review uncertainty.
- Payment account, payer/payee, purpose wording, refund mechanism, withholding responsibility, or tax filing evidence is missing or inconsistent.
- Registration change, payment release, and tax filing sequence is unclear.

Possible controls: tax filing as condition precedent, withholding covenant, gross-up or refund mechanism, supervised account, bank pre-review, escrow release condition, payment-registration sequence, and completion evidence for tax and FX steps.

### Hard Procedure Blocks

- A triggered approval, filing, public listing, disclosure, shareholder approval, third-party consent, merger-control filing, national security review, or industry permit amendment is not completed.
- The parties treat a mandatory procedure as a post-closing covenant even though it may affect legality, registration, disclosure, control, or payment release.

Possible controls: hard condition precedent, long-stop date, no-waiver clause, closing deferral, regulatory termination right, reverse termination allocation, and official-source verification.

### Evidence And Process

- Key documents are unsigned, unstamped, incomplete, inconsistent, unreadable, OCR-only, or contradicted by public registry data.
- Entity names, dates, percentages, consideration, account details, resolutions, or appendices conflict.
- Platform finding is not yet official-source verified.

Possible controls: source register, conflict table, original-file request, notarized copy, screenshot archive, certified registry file, and open verification item.

## Transaction Control Matrix

| Risk type | First control to consider |
|---|---|
| Legal block | Condition precedent or pre-closing remediation. |
| Evidence gap | Targeted supplemental materials and source register. |
| Monetary exposure | Holdback, escrow, price adjustment, or special indemnity. |
| Registration uncertainty | Closing sequence and long-stop termination. |
| Hidden liability | Disclosure schedule, warranty survival, and indemnity carve-out. |
| Regulatory uncertainty | Official verification and filing/approval timetable. |
| Third-party consent | Consent as condition precedent with fallback termination right. |
| Contribution exposure | Contribution cure, price deduction, holdback, and special indemnity. |
| Tax or FX completion | Filing/payment evidence, withholding covenant, bank pre-review, and escrow release condition. |
| Mandatory procedure | Hard condition precedent, no-waiver rule, and long-stop termination. |
